About Falah Academy

Kent's first full-time Islamic school — built by the community, for the community.

Our Story

Falah Academy was established to meet a long-felt need in the Muslim community — a full-time Islamic school where children receive a strong academic foundation without compromising their Islamic identity. We believe that a child's education should reflect their faith, their values, and their potential.

At the heart of everything we do is a deep sense of responsibility toward the young Ummah of Nabi Muhammad ﷺ. These children are an amanah — a trust — and we are committed to raising them as confident Muslims who know who they are, where they come from, and what they stand for. We want them to walk into any room, in any part of the world, grounded in their faith and ready to contribute meaningfully to the society around them.

Nurturing Knowledge in the House of Allah

All classes at Falah Academy are held within the serene and sacred environment of Al Huda Islamic Center — 25650 101st Ave SE, Kent, WA 98030. We consider this a great blessing.

Our students begin and end each school day in the House of Allah, surrounded by an environment that reminds them of their purpose and their responsibility as young Muslims.

Our Mission

We are committed to providing students with an education that is both academically rigorous and deeply rooted in Islamic values. Through a carefully designed dual curriculum, we prepare our students not just for the next grade level, but for a life of purpose, character, and contribution.

We see in every child the potential to be a future leader, a caring neighbor, a productive citizen, and a proud Muslim. Our goal is to nurture that potential — to graduate young men and women who carry the values of the Prophet ﷺ in their hearts, confidence in their steps, and a genuine desire to make a difference in the world around them.

Our Vision

Falah Academy is in its early years, but our vision is long-term. We are building a school that will grow with this community — adding grade levels, deepening our programs, and becoming a trusted institution for Muslim families across Kent and beyond.

We began with Pre-K and Kindergarten, and we are proud to be growing — introducing Grade 1 as we continue to build something meaningful, one year at a time.
